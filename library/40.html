If the preferences of the experts regarding their presentation times are as follows: - E1 prefers to present between 1 PM and 3 PM. - E2 cannot present before 2 PM. - E3 needs exactly 2 hours for his talk. - E4 prefers to present in the last hour of the conference. - E5 is flexible and can present at any time. question:Matthew Phillips, a renowned musician, releases a new album every 1.5 years. A passionate music journalist wants to analyze the popularity trajectory of his albums using a mathematical model. She collects data over a period of 15 years, noting that the popularity of each album follows a sinusoidal pattern due to trends and seasonal variations in music preferences. The popularity of the first album, released at ( t = 0 ) years, is given by the function ( P(t) = 50 + 30sin(2pi(t - 0.5)) ), where ( P(t) ) represents the popularity score at time ( t ) in years.1. Derive a general expression for the popularity ( P_n(t) ) of the ( n )-th album released by Matthew Phillips, assuming it follows the same sinusoidal pattern but is shifted by 1.5 years for each subsequent album.2. Using the derived expression, calculate the total cumulative popularity score of all albums released over the 15-year period.

answer:Let's solve the problem step-by-step. 1. Derive a general expression for the popularity ( P_n(t) ) of the ( n )-th album.Given the popularity function for the first album is:[ P(t) = 50 + 30sin(2pi(t - 0.5)) ]The ( n )-th album is released ( n-1 ) intervals of 1.5 years after the first album. Therefore, the ( n )-th album is released at:[ t = (n-1) times 1.5 text{ years} ]The general form of the popularity function for the ( n )-th album, ( P_n(t) ), would be shifted by ( (n-1) times 1.5 ) years. So, we adjust the argument of the sine function:[ P_n(t) = 50 + 30sinleft(2pileft(t - 0.5 - (n-1) times 1.5right)right) ]Simplify the argument of the sine function:[ t - 0.5 - (n-1) times 1.5 = t - 0.5 - 1.5(n-1) = t - 0.5 - 1.5n + 1.5 = t - 1.5n + 1 ]So the expression becomes:[ P_n(t) = 50 + 30sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) ] 2. Calculate the total cumulative popularity score of all albums released over the 15-year period.To find the total cumulative popularity score, we need to sum the popularity scores of all albums at various time points. The musician releases an album every 1.5 years over 15 years, so the number of albums released in 15 years is:[ frac{15}{1.5} = 10 text{ albums} ]We need to sum the popularity scores of these 10 albums over the 15 years. The cumulative popularity score ( S(t) ) at any time ( t ) is:[ S(t) = sum_{n=1}^{10} P_n(t) ]Using the derived expression for ( P_n(t) ):[ P_n(t) = 50 + 30sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) ]So,[ S(t) = sum_{n=1}^{10} left[ 50 + 30sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) right] ]Separate the sum:[ S(t) = sum_{n=1}^{10} 50 + sum_{n=1}^{10} 30sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) ][ S(t) = 50 times 10 + 30 sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) ][ S(t) = 500 + 30 sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) ]To find the total cumulative popularity score over the entire 15-year period, we need to integrate ( S(t) ) over ( t ) from 0 to 15:[ text{Total cumulative score} = int_{0}^{15} S(t) , dt ][ = int_{0}^{15} left[ 500 + 30 sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) right] dt ]Separate the integral:[ = int_{0}^{15} 500 , dt + 30 int_{0}^{15} sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) , dt ][ = 500 times 15 + 30 int_{0}^{15} sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) , dt ][ = 7500 + 30 int_{0}^{15} sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) , dt ]The integral of the sinusoidal function over a complete number of cycles averages to zero. Since the sine function completes multiple cycles over the 15-year period, the integral of the sine terms will average out to zero:[ int_{0}^{15} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) , dt = 0 ]Thus,[ 30 int_{0}^{15} sum_{n=1}^{10} sin(2pi(t - 1.5n + 1)) , dt = 0 ]Therefore, the total cumulative popularity score over the 15-year period is:[ 7500 + 0 = 7500 ]Final Answer: The final answer is 7500. question:A retired police officer, who loves indulging in comfort foods and is resistant to change, has a specific routine that involves visiting his favorite diner every day. He always orders the same three comfort foods: a burger, fries, and a milkshake. The cost of the burger, fries, and milkshake are B, F, and M respectively. If inflation causes the prices of the burger, fries, and milkshake to increase by 5%, 3%, and 4% respectively after the first 6 months, determine the officer’s total expenditure on these comfort foods by the end of the year.

answer:Let's solve each part of the problem step-by-step. Part 1: Determine the Individual Costs of the Burger, Fries, and Milkshake1. Given: - Every week the officer spends 224 on these comfort foods. - The officer visits the diner every day of the week (7 days). - Each day he orders 1 burger, 1 fries, and 1 milkshake. Therefore, in one week, he orders: - 7 burgers - 7 fries - 7 milkshakes The total weekly cost equation is: [ 7B + 7F + 7M = 224 ] Simplify this equation by dividing everything by 7: [ B + F + M = 32 ]2. Use the given relationships: - The burger costs twice as much as the fries: ( B = 2F ) - The cost of the milkshake is 5 more than the fries: ( M = F + 5 )3. Substitute these relationships into the total cost equation: [ B + F + M = 32 ] Substitute ( B = 2F ) and ( M = F + 5 ): [ 2F + F + (F + 5) = 32 ] Simplify: [ 4F + 5 = 32 ] Solve for ( F ): [ 4F = 27 ] [ F = frac{27}{4} = 6.75 ]4. Find the costs of the burger and milkshake: - Burger: ( B = 2F = 2 times 6.75 = 13.50 ) - Milkshake: ( M = F + 5 = 6.75 + 5 = 11.75 ) Part 2: Calculate the Total Amount Spent Over a Year with Inflation1. Initial weekly cost: The weekly cost is given as 224. For the first 6 months (26 weeks), the total expenditure is: [ 224 times 26 = 5824 ]2. Inflation-adjusted prices: After 6 months, the prices increase: - Burger (5% increase): ( B = 13.50 times 1.05 = 14.175 ) - Fries (3% increase): ( F = 6.75 times 1.03 = 6.9525 ) - Milkshake (4% increase): ( M = 11.75 times 1.04 = 12.22 )3. New weekly cost after 6 months: Calculate the new total weekly cost with the increased prices: [ text{New weekly cost} = 7 times (14.175 + 6.9525 + 12.22) ] Simplify inside the parentheses: [ 14.175 + 6.9525 + 12.22 = 33.3475 ] Then: [ text{New weekly cost} = 7 times 33.3475 = 233.4325 ]4. Total expenditure for the remaining 26 weeks: [ 233.4325 times 26 = 6069.245 ]5.
